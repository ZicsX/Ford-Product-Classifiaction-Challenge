Company background

Under its French name, Parme, it was also created a duché grand-fief de l'Empire for Charles-François Lebrun, duc de Plaisance, the Emperor's Arch-Treasurer, on 24 April 1808 (extinguished in 1926). After the foreigners were expelled, Parma belonged to the Papal States until 1545. Parma's Etruscan name was adapted by Romans to describe the round shield called Parma.
